Chicago Public Library

CPL's 81 branch locations are more than libraries — they're community resource hubs that anyone can walk into without knowing exactly what they need. No appointment required.

No library card needed to walk in. If you don't have a fixed address, ask for a free temporary 90-day card. You can also use computers, Wi-Fi, and most resources without a card.

What branches offer

  • Free computer and internet access
  • Help navigating 2-1-1 and 3-1-1 resources
  • Job application and resume assistance
  • Notary services at many locations
  • Safe, climate-controlled daytime space — no questions asked
  • Community events, classes, and programs

Select branches with more services

Harold Washington Library Center (400 S State St, Loop) — The main branch. Most comprehensive services including legal self-help resources, expanded computer lab, and social services referrals.

Uptown Branch (929 W Buena Ave) — On-site social worker. Serves a community with high need for housing and social service navigation.

Free health vending machines

Select branches have free public health vending machines stocked with: menstrual products, hygiene kits, socks, underwear, Narcan (naloxone), and drug test strips. Call your nearest branch to ask if they have one.
